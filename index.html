The Big 3 of Anime

One Piece • Naruto • Bleach

The Big 3 of Anime refers to One Piece, Naruto, and Bleach—three legendary shōnen series that dominated anime culture during the early 2000s. Running simultaneously in Weekly Shōnen Jump, these shows defined a generation and introduced millions to anime worldwide.

Their powerful storytelling, emotional themes, and long-running adventures helped anime become a global phenomenon. Even today, their legacy strongly influences modern anime.

One Piece

Created by Eiichiro Oda in 1997, One Piece follows Monkey D. Luffy on his quest to become the Pirate King. The series is famous for its massive world-building, memorable characters, and themes of freedom and loyalty.

As the best-selling manga of all time, One Piece continues to expand its story and remains a cornerstone of anime history.

Naruto

Naruto, created by Masashi Kishimoto, tells the story of Naruto Uzumaki, a young ninja seeking recognition and peace. Debuting in 1999, the series focuses on perseverance, friendship, and overcoming pain.

Naruto’s emotional storytelling and iconic moments made it one of the most influential anime series ever created.

Bleach

Created by Tite Kubo in 2001, Bleach follows Ichigo Kurosaki, a teenager who gains Soul Reaper powers. The anime is known for its stylish visuals, intense battles, and darker supernatural themes.

Bleach’s return with the Thousand-Year Blood War arc reaffirmed its lasting popularity and legacy.

Together, the Big 3 of Anime shaped the modern anime industry and inspired millions of fans across the world. Their stories remain timeless, and their influence will continue for generations to come.
